Visualizing Women's Advancement:
A Global View of Women's Educational, Financial, and Social Wellbeing

Jose DelValle - Claudia Durkin - Lizzie Friend - Maria Manna - MaKenzie Muller
This project is a visualization of women's advancement on a global scale, encompassing key areas such as health, education, marriage, labor and financial opportunities. Utilizing data from the World Bank Gender Data Portal and U.S. Maternal Education datasets, this project aims to provide users with insights on gender differences worldwide and specific insights within the United States. There are potential issues of data inequity, with missing data for lower-income countries and those with economic instability due to war, famine, and the lasting impacts of the Covid-19 pandemic.
These visualizations offer users an interactive experience, allowing them to explore and analyze patterns in women's lives across different regions and over several decades. While the dashboards are presented in separate sections, there are promising trends across all indicators.
In some regions, women have surpassed men in educational attainment. Maternal health outcomes have improved significantly, marked by reduced adolescent pregnancies, delayed motherhood and higher education for mothers, with the United States being a prime example of these advancements. There are encouraging strides toward marriage rights and financial inclusion. Women now have greater autonomy in choosing when to marry and benefit from improved access to bank accounts and financial opportunities, fostering economic empowerment.
Despite these positive developments, specific regions, such as Sub-Saharan Africa and the Middle East, face persistent challenges. These regions continue to grapple with disparities in education, maternal health, and women's financial and marriage rights.
Overall, the visualizations underscore the importance of acknowledging the successes and the remaining obstacles in women's advancement globally. Highlighting these trends, we hope to inspire greater advocacy to address gender disparities and promote women's rights and well-being across all regions.
