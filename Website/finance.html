Income and Social Security Dashboard

This financial dashboard offers a detailed analysis of how median income and social security benefits influence fentanyl deaths across various California counties. The dashboard reveals a notable trend: counties with larger social security benefits tend to report higher numbers of fentanyl-related fatalities. However, this correlation may be influenced by the fact that larger benefits typically occur in more populous counties, which naturally might experience more deaths due to their size. Interestingly, the data also shows that the median income of a county does not appear to correlate with fentanyl deaths, indicating that the fentanyl crisis impacts individuals across all income levels equally. This dashboard provides crucial insights into the economic factors that may or may not be influencing the spread of the opioid crisis, helping policymakers and public health officials to better understand the socioeconomic dimensions of this epidemic.

How to Use This Dashboard

Utilize the slider at the bottom of the dashboard to select a specific year; the data displayed will automatically update to reflect that year's statistics, enabling observation of trends over time. For a detailed view, hover your mouse over any point in the scatterplot to see a tooltip with the county's name, its average median income, social security benefits, and the number of fentanyl deaths. This interactive functionality allows users to analyze how economic factors correlate with fentanyl fatalities, providing insights at both a broad and granular level.
