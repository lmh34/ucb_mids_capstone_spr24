Social and Population Dashboards



Population Dashboard

Explore our visualization that compares county populations with fentanyl-related fatalities across California, and includes a breakdown of fentanyl deaths by age groups among the male population per county. The first component illustrates the relationship between the population size of each county and the corresponding number of fentanyl deaths, identifying geographic trends within the state. Additionally, a second chart details the impact on different male age groups, supporting targeted public health responses. This analysis provides insights into the fentanyl crisis's impact in relation to population dynamics and highlights demographic factors, helping policymakers, healthcare providers, and community leaders to allocate resources and design interventions effectively.

How to Use This Dashboard

This dashboard allows users to analyze the relationship between county populations, fentanyl-related fatalities, and age demographic data across California. Utilize the County Name Dropdown Filter to select a specific county, updating all dashboard components to display data for that area. The Population Group Filter enables examination of data within specific demographic segments for targeted insights, while the Date Range Slider allows adjustment of the timeframe to explore trends over time. These interactive tools enable users to tailor the visualization to their specific needs, facilitating a deeper understanding of the patterns and impacts of fentanyl-related deaths within selected parameters.


Social Media Sentiment Dashboard

How does a county's sentiment affect the fentanyl death count?

Discover insights through our interactive visualization that compares social media sentiment ratings from Twitter with monthly fentanyl death counts across various counties in California, spanning from December 2020 to December 2022. This visualization quantifies Twitter sentiment on a scale from 0 to 1, where a score close to 0 indicates predominantly negative sentiments and a score close to 1 reflects more positive sentiments.

Our analysis highlights a significant trend: while the number of fentanyl-related fatalities consistently increases, counties that show a decline in sentiment scores on Twitter tend to see a sharper rise in death counts. This correlation suggests that public sentiment on social media may serve as an early indicator of escalating fentanyl crises

How to Use This Dashboard

To navigate, use the month slider at the bottom of the dashboard to select a specific period, or choose a county either from the dropdown list or by clicking directly on the county in the map to focus the displayed data. These actions will update the charts to show changes in sentiment scores and death counts accordingly.
